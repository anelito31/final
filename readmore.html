QAZAQSTAN IN ANCIENT TIMES

In the first centuries of the 1st millennium AD, the nomadic Scythian-Saka civilization flourished on the territory of the steppe zone of Central Asia, the cultural monuments of which have survived to this day.

The tomb of the Golden Saka warrior, discovered in the settlement of Issyk, not far from Almaty, is famous for its integrity, beauty and grace.

In subsequent centuries, a powerful Hunnic power was formed in the steppes of modern Kazakhstan, which had a huge influence on the geopolitical map of the world of that era. The Great Roman Empire fell under the blows of the Hunnic warriors of Attila.

EDUCATION OF THE QAZAQ NATION

The first Kazakh khanates began to form, and by the first half of the 16th century, the formation of a single nation was completed.

The ethnonym "Kazakh" translated from the ancient Turkic language means "free, independent" and fully reflects the character of the people, who at all times strive for an independent, independent existence.
